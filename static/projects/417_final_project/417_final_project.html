The final project for CSCI 417: Computer Animation

  • Meg Anderson and Alex Fantine
  • 2020.05.10
  • Project 7 - Final Project

This was our final project for computer animation. We wanted to make an animation about this past semester, but with a humorous take on the Covid pandemic. Our animation is about one of the only things that might be happy we're all trapped at home: a roomba.

Storyboard and Description


The story starts with a frustrated Roomba. He has nothing to do. That is, until he hears something. He turns around to find a trash ball. He's excited, and sucks it up. But then he turns to find several more, leading out of the kitchen. He rounds the corner in the living room, and discovers a trash mountain. Looks like there's been some sort of netflix marathon. The final shot is of him caught in the trash, absolutely thrilled to be caught in the mess.


The story ends with a freeze frame on the roomba, king of the trash hill.

Principles of Animation

We used several principles of animation in this project. The first is anticipation. The roomba hits the wall, bouncing back each time. Until the ball of trash hits the floor. There's a delay, and then he turns around. He pauses before vacuuming up the trash, considering it.

The second principle used was staging. When the animation starts, the roomba is not at the center of the screen. He is off to the side. This is intentional; when the trash drops it becomes clear the roomba wasn't the focus of the scene, the trash, which lands center at the corner of the fridge, was. Similarly, the TV scene is staged to place focus on the trash.

The roomba's movement follows slow-in slow-out. He doesn't move at top speed right away. Instead, he takes a minute to get going, and builds speed before decelerating.

The animation uses squash and stretch. The roomba's bumper is animated, so it takes inital impact, squishing against the roomba. Similarly, the first trash ball that falls squishes when it hits the ground, stretches when it bounces back.

There's also a cute secondary action in the movement of the roomba. The spinning of the brushes as he moves adds to the personality of our main character. It doesn't distract from the animation, but just adds a little life to the roomba. This adds to the roomba's overall appeal. His little smile, and the overall soft cheery color palette of the animation, add to the animation's overall appeal.
