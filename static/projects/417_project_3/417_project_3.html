This was the third project, using Disney's 12 Principles of Animation to animate a bouncing ball.

Storyline

My storyline? Truly shakespearean. A story of betrayal. Meet our two characters, Pinky, a very good boy, and naughty, untrustworthy Golf.




The basic storyline is that Pinky out-bounces Golf. In a fit of jealous rage, Golf pushes Pinky from the table.

Principles

There were four (primary) principles I played with:

  • Squash and stretch
  • Anticipation
  • Staging
  • Arcs

Squash and stretch
This one is pretty straight forward. I wanted to show a difference in our two characters based on how they deform on hitting the table. We "like" Pinky more because he feels more elastic and nice, while Golf does not deform much on hitting the table.


Anticipation
There are several moments of anticipation in the animation. A regret of mine is that I didn't stretch out the anticipation even further, but que sera. We have a moment of wondering: will Golf do it? What goes on in that round head of his? And then there is the moment when Golf looks back, over his shoulder, and we wonder: what is he looking at?




Staging
We love the rule of thirds. Here it is simple and effective. I also choose to have Pinky fly in so that he is partially out of frame, to make his reappearance more surprising.
