This was the fourth project, using Disney's 12 Principles of Animation to animate a roller coaster.

Storyline


The basic storyline is that its a roller coaster in a cute forest, and the camera tries to anticipate the movements.

Principles

There were three principles I used:

  • Anticipation
  • Slow-in Slow-out
  • Appeal

Anticipation
I keyed the camera to look at where the track is headed before it reaches it. I also had the car stall at a few critical moments, such as at the coaster's peak or on the loop.


Slow-in Slow-Out
As you can see from the graph editor I've included screenshots of, the car slowly gains velocity and slowly loses it.


Appeal
I spent more time than I should have building my forest. Originally, the camera was meant ot walk up to the platform through the forest, I just ran out of time to key two motion paths. The forest has little toadstools and what I am warmly calling "bubble trees"



Arcs
My coaster track is built entirely of CV curves, with a piece of track I created animation snap-shot-ed along the track and then bridges together. The coaster is meant to have nice gentle arcs.
