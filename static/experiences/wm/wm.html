At William & Mary, I also lead our ACM chapter for two years as Chair, and for one year as hackathon chair. ACM was my deepest and most meaningful involvement at William & Mary. During my time as chair, I saw (and hope I fostered) a real change in the culture of the CS department at William & Mary. It became a more welcoming and inclusive space. The classes were no less challenging, but ACM was responsible for deepening the connections between students and faculty, making those hard classes bearable. I also acted as a student perspective in departmental decisions, such as hiring new faculty and redesigning the McGlothlin Street Hall Computer Science Lab.

During my time as Chair I also had a lot of fun. I also helped to plan tribeHacks IV, Cypher V, and Cypher VI William & Mary’s annual hackathon attended by over 200 hackers, with an annual budget of over $20,000, but more importantly a yearly Costco run that requires four cars.
